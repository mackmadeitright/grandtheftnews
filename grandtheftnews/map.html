The GTA 6 Map

Vice City is back — and this time it's part of a whole state. Here's what's actually confirmed about the world of Grand Theft Auto VI.

Where is GTA 6 set?

Grand Theft Auto VI is set in Vice City and the surrounding state of Leonida — Rockstar's fictionalized version of modern-day Miami and Florida. Vice City itself is the central urban environment, a neon-drenched reimagining of Miami, while Leonida extends the world outward into the kind of varied terrain Florida is known for: coastline and beaches, wetlands and swamps, and smaller towns beyond the city limits.

How big is the GTA 6 map?

Rockstar has not published official square-mileage figures. Based on trailers, screenshots, and community mapping projects, the consensus estimate places Leonida at roughly 1.5 to 2 times the size of GTA V's map, with a notably higher density of enterable buildings and environmental variety. Treat specific size numbers as informed estimates rather than confirmed facts until Rockstar states otherwise.

What regions and locations are in the map?

From what's been officially shown, the world spans several distinct environments:

  • Vice City: the central metropolis — beaches, Art Deco districts, nightlife, and dense urban streets.
  • Coastline and beaches: the Miami-inspired waterfront that the series' Vice City is famous for.
  • Wetlands and swamps: Everglades-style natural areas extending the map well beyond the city.
  • Smaller towns and rural stretches: the wider state of Leonida filling in around the urban core.

Rockstar has released official location reveals and 4K screenshots that flesh out these areas. As more of the map is officially detailed — especially once gameplay footage arrives — we'll expand this page.

Is there an official GTA 6 map image?

Rockstar has not released a full labeled overworld map of the kind players pore over before launch. What exists publicly are official screenshots and location art, plus fan-made maps assembled from trailer analysis. Be cautious with "leaked full maps" circulating online — many are speculative reconstructions rather than official material.
